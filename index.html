MY OPERATING SYSTEM

I build through
human-driven innovation.

My work sits at the intersection of design, technology, communication, strategy, and education—but people are always at the center of it.

people first

I approach innovation as my mentor and Department Lead, Joyce Pereira, defines it:

“Innovation is creatively using available knowledge and resources to meet human needs and enhance human experiences.” — Joyce Pereira

That philosophy shapes the way I solve problems.

I do not use design simply to make something attractive. I use it to create clarity, connection, and understanding.

I do not use technology simply because it is new. I use it when it can make an experience more useful, accessible, efficient, or engaging.

I do not rely on one discipline to solve every problem. I draw from design, programming, marketing, data, storytelling, education, and engineering to build the solution the situation actually requires.

The tools may change.
The purpose does not:

Understand the human need. Use what is available. Build thoughtfully. Improve the experience.

IN THE INTEREST OF FULL TRANSPARENCY

I do have a few red flags.

  • I get hangry.
  • I occasionally forget to text people back.
  • I sometimes convince myself that everyone hates me when, in reality, I am simply overthinking and probably need food.

Nobody is perfectly user-friendly.

THE POINT OF ALL THIS

I do not believe people are flat.

I do not believe the best ideas come from staying inside one discipline.

And I do not believe professionalism requires hiding every interesting part of yourself.

My personality, lived experiences, cultural background, motherhood, curiosity, humor, technical skills, design instincts, communication style, and capacity for empathy all influence the way I work.

That is what human-driven innovation means to me.

It means seeing the full person behind the problem.

It means using what you know—and being willing to learn what you do not.

It means building with empathy, purpose, resourcefulness, and care.

It means remembering that every interface, system, message, classroom, product, and experience eventually reaches a human being.

I build for that human.
